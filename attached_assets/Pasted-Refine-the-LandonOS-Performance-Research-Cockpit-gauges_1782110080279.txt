Refine the LandonOS Performance Research Cockpit gauges and automotive UI elements so they look realistic, premium, and luxury-performance inspired.

Problem to fix:
The current gauges / car-inspired elements look too cartoony. They should not feel like a game, toy dashboard, or generic racing graphic.

Design goal:
Make the automotive elements feel like a real luxury performance car cockpit: refined, precise, metallic, dimensional, and executive.

Gauge style requirements:
- Use realistic luxury instrument-cluster styling
- Dark graphite or black gauge faces
- Subtle carbon fiber or brushed-metal texture
- Thin chrome / silver outer rings
- Fine tick marks
- Clean numeric labels
- Red needle or red progress arc
- Soft inner glow, not neon overload
- Subtle glass reflection or sheen
- Depth through shadows and layering
- Smooth circular progress arcs
- No chunky cartoon rings
- No oversized playful icons
- No toy-like speedometer graphics
- No flame/checkered-flag styling

Use gauges for:
- Report Readiness
- Source Quality
- Research Progress
- Training Progress
- Reward / Level Progress
- Human Review Risk

Specific visual direction:
Think modern Ferrari / Porsche / Lamborghini / Mercedes AMG digital cockpit inspiration, but do not use any real brand logos or copyrighted dashboard replicas. The result should be original and brand-safe, inspired by luxury performance design.

Keep it professional:
This is still an executive compliance/business research app. The gauges should support decision-making, not overpower the workspace.

Implementation requirements:
- Replace cartoony gauge components with realistic SVG/CSS or canvas-based instrument gauges.
- Use layered circles, thin tick marks, subtle gradients, metallic borders, and red accent arcs.
- Add labels that are readable and not cramped.
- Use high contrast for numbers and status text.
- Keep gauges compact enough for dashboard use.
- Ensure gauges work on desktop, tablet, and mobile.
- Do not use external car brand assets.
- Do not use stock images as gauges.
- Do not use low-quality emoji/icons for automotive styling.

Recommended gauge component structure:
- Outer metallic ring
- Inner graphite face
- Fine tick marks around edge
- Red progress arc or needle
- Center value
- Small status label
- Optional micro warning badge

Examples:
- Report Readiness: circular gauge showing 72%
- Source Quality: gauge showing Strong / Needs Source
- Research Progress: route/progress gauge
- Training Progress: level meter
- Human Review Risk: red caution gauge, but still refined

Also refine:
- Track Map should feel like a premium navigation/race telemetry route, not a cartoon racetrack.
- Pit Stop should feel like a professional blocker/help workflow with subtle automotive language, not a racing game.
- Garage Rewards should feel like a luxury performance achievement system, not childish gamification.

Avoid:
- Cartoon dashboards
- Toy speedometers
- Big playful icons
- Flames
- Excessive checkered flags
- Neon overload
- Low-contrast red-on-black text
- Fake car brand logos
- Busy decorative backgrounds
- Anything that makes the app less readable

After implementing:
- Compare the gauges against the design goal.
- Confirm they look premium and realistic.
- Confirm all gauge labels are readable.
- Confirm the app still feels executive and research-focused.
- Run typecheck/build.
- Check desktop and mobile layouts.
- Provide before/after notes describing how the gauges were improved.
